# Day1 - CRM Basics

## 1. What is CRM?
CRM stands for Customer Relationship Management. It is a technology that helps organizations manage relationships with their customers and prospects, and track all interactions with them.

CRM systems allow teams to collaborate effectively, both internally and externally. They also help gather insights from social media, track important business metrics, and communicate through multiple channels such as email, phone, and social platforms.

In Salesforce, all this information is securely stored in the cloud. This makes it easy for users to access and update data from anywhere. To understand this better, we can compare it to a spreadsheet, where data is organized in rows and columns, but Salesforce provides a much more powerful, secure, and scalable system for managing customer information.

---

## 2. Why companies use Salesforce?
Companies use Salesforce because:
- It stores customer data securely
- Helps sales teams track leads and opportunities
- Improves communication with customers
- Automates business processes
- Generates reports and dashboards
- Works on cloud, so it can be accessed anywhere
- Increases productivity and sales growth

# 3. Important Salesforce Objects

## Account
An Account represents a company, organization, or customer business.

Example:
- Infosys
- TCS
- ABC College

---

## Contact
A Contact is a person related to an account.

Example:
- Student name
- Customer name
- Employee name

---

## Opportunity
An Opportunity represents a possible business deal or sale.

Example:
- Student admission process
- Product purchase deal
- Event ticket booking

---

# 4. Business Flow
## Lead → Contact → Opportunity → Customer

### Lead
A lead is a new person who shows interest in a business or service.

Example:
A student fills out a college admission inquiry form.

### Contact
After verification, the lead becomes a contact with proper details stored in the system.

Example:
The college stores the student’s details like name, phone number, and course interest.

### Opportunity
An opportunity is a potential deal or chance of success.

Example:
The student is interested in joining the college and the admission process is ongoing.

### Customer
When the deal is completed successfully, the person becomes a customer.

Example:
The student pays the fee and officially joins the college.
